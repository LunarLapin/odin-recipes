Baked Potato

Description

I came to appreciate baked potatoes several years ago. Many who enjoy this delicious dish know the difference between a good baked potato and a great baked potato. The crispiness of the skin, the hint of crunchy salt, and the light and fluffy insides are heaven on a plate. Adding a dollop of sour cream, chives, or any other topping of choice on top of the steamy fluff make this a comforting delight.

Ingredients

  • Russet potatoes
  • Butter or olive oil
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly-ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 450 degrees F. This will help achieve the ideal crispiness
  2. Prepare baking sheet. Place potatoes on a foil lined baking sheet
  3. Poke holes all over each potato with a fork
  4. First bake. You'll be baking the potatoes twice. For the first round, bake for 25 minutes or until the skin feels dry and slightly wrinkly
  5. Brush the potato with melted butter (or oil). carefully remove from the oven and use a pastry brush to brush with melted butter or oil evenly on all sides. Sprinkle with a generous pinch of Kosher salt. Place back on baking sheet, opposite-side up.
  6. Second Bake. Use an oven mit and gently squeeze the potatoes to check if the insides are nice and soft. If they do not seem soft enough, continue cooking in 5-minute increments.
